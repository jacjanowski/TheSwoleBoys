Dumbbell Single-Arm Row Lat Pulldowns Farmer's Walk Barbell Bent-Over Row Pull-up Inverted Row

Description: A great and simple exercise for targeting the lats. Its slow movement allows you to concentrate on the exercises form well.

Instructions
  1. Get a dumbbell of a desired weight and a flat bench. Place your right hand on the bench directly underneath your shoulder. Place your right knee on the bench.
  2. Grasp the dumbbell in your left hand and row it up until your upper arm is parallel to the floor.
  3. Lower the left arm slowly to the floor without dropping the shoulders and while keeping the torso locked.
  4. Repeat on the other arm with left knee and left hand on flat bench and right arm doing the lifting.

Description: An isometric workout designed to effectively target the lats. This workout is very commonly seen performed by body-builders

Instructions
  1. At the lat pulldown machine, grab the bar with a wide, overhand grip. Set the desired weight on the machine.
  2. Grasp the bar from a standing position and sit down on the bench.
  3. In one motion, pull the bar down to around chin level, exhaling during the pull down. Keep shoulders locked and squeeze the shoulder blades together.
  4. At the bottom of the position, gently bring the bar back to original position.

Description: A very simple exercise that targets hand grip-strength and the entire back.

Instructions
  1. Choose a pair of dumbbells of desired weight. Hold both dumbbells by your sides in a standing position.
  2. Walk short, quick steps while maintaining tension in arms.

Description: A very effective exercise at targeting the whole of the upper back while recruiting muscles from other muscle groups.

Instructions
  1. Grab a barbell with overhand grip and bring it to a standing position. Bend-the upper body forwards 45 degrees while keeping shoulder blades back, legs slightly bent, and back completely straight.
  2. Row the barbell towards you into the lower part of the chest, exhaling as you row.
  3. At the top, pause then gently bring barbell back down to the resting position.

Description: An intermediate workout which will target the lats directly. This workout is great for those wanting the v-shape physique

Instructions
  1. Grab the handles or a pull-up bar with plams facing away and arms fully extended. Hands should be about shoulder-width apart.
  2. Squeeze the shoulder blades together, exhale, and bring your elbows down to your hips. This should raise your chin above the bar.
  3. Lower your body down gently to the resting position.

Description: A great exercise for targeting the arms, back, and grip-strength. This exercise can be used by a novice to build-up to a pull-up.

Instructions
  1. Place a barbell on a squat rack such that it is stable.
  2. Position yourself under the bar such that when you lie down, the bar is an arms-length away.
  3. From a lie-down position, grasp the bar and pull the chest to the bar, while squeezing the shoulder blades together and keeping elbows in.
  4. At the top of the motion, pause, and then gently lower back down.
