Description: The bench press builds the muscles of the chest as well as the triceps of the back of the arms and the front deltoid shoulder muscles.

Instructions
  1. Lie on the bench under the rack that holds the bar. Your eyes should be approximately aligned with the front of the barbell rack uprights. Butt, shoulders, and head should be flat on the bench with a slight (neutral) bend in the spine.
  2. Grasp the bar with your thumbs on the outside of your closed fist, overhand grip, with arms slightly wider than shoulder-width apart. The angle of the upper arms should be at about 45 degrees to the body.

Description: Cable flys are extemely verstaile and allow you to expand the width of your chest. Able to perform straight, incline, and decline flys that work all around the pectoral area.

Instructions
  1. Stand between two high pulley cables with stirrup attachments.
  2. Hold left handle in left hand and right handle in right hand with an overhand grip (palms facing down). Stand upright in a staggered stance with arms outstretched but slightly bent so that knuckles are facing forward.. This is the starting position.
  3. Begin exercise by pushing cables forward and together so that your wrists cross when arms are fully extended. Pause, then slowly reverse movement back to starting position. This completes one rep.

Description: The dumbbell press activates the chest and shoulders and helps to strengthen your form much more due to the individual grip for each dumbbell.

Instructions
  1. Lie back on a bench holding a dumbbell in each hand just to the sides of your shoulders. Your palms should be facing towards your feet in the starting position, although if you have shoulder issues then switch to a neutral grip, where the palms face each other.
  2. Press the weights above your chest by extending your elbows until your arms are straight, then bring the weights back down slowly. To take advantage of the range of movement offered by using dumbbells rather than a barbell, take the weights down past your shoulders and bring them closer together at the top of the movement.

Description: Throwing a mild incline into the mix allows for greater range of motion to attack the chest muscles far more effectively than the standard bench.

Instructions
  1. Pick up the dumbbells off the floor using a neutral grip (palms facing in). Position the ends of the dumbbells in your hip crease, and sit down on the edge of an incline bench.
  2. To get into position, lay back and keep the weights close to your chest. Once you are in position, take a deep breath, and press the dumbbells to lockout at the top.
  3. Slowly lower the dumbbells under control as far as comfortably possible (the handles should be about level with your chest).
  4. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

Description: The seated chest press machine is an upright version of the standard lying bench press machine. The arms, placed under a weight-bearing load, are pushed away from the chest and returned to starting position

Instructions
  1. Sit so feet are flat on floor, knees are above ankles, back is straight and supported on pad.
  2. Grip the handles near your chest, palms facing downward. Engage abs and keep spine straight.
  3. When pushing the handles forward, make sure the elbows do not lock. Aim for a full range of motion, but don’t allow the weight stack to slam back down.

Description: The pec deck works both your chest and its supporting muscles, primarily your pectoralis major, which is the muscle that allows you to swing and bring your arms together

Instructions
  1. Bend your arms at a 90° angle and keep your elbows at chest level. Gripping the pec deck handles, pull your arms toward your body while contracting your pectoral muscles.
  2. Bring the handles or arm pads in front of your chest, hold the position for a couple of seconds, and then slowly release back to starting position.
  3. Gripping the pec deck handles, pull your arms toward your body while contracting your pectoral muscles. Bring the handles or arm pads in front of your chest, hold the position for a couple of seconds, and then slowly release back to starting position.
